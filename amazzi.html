About the Amazzi Wellness Project

Many of the locals fetch water for domestic use out of Lake Victoria. In these shallow shore waters inhabitants also bathe, wash clothes, and step as they board and offload boats. Animals including dogs, cattle, goats, and others also drink from these waters. It is dirty, contaminated, and unsafe for human use. But without any other source of water, they have no choice but to resort to lake water.

Many times, it is the little children that fetch water. In a bid to get cleaner water, the children are sometimes compelled to move further into the lake to draw from deeper waters, which poses a danger to them. Sometimes, a thick green alga totally covers huge portions of the lake water. During such seasons, the inhabitants have no access to water for domestic use at all. They resort to buying water from boatmen who fetch it from other islands at a high cost per jerrycan.

Through the Amazzi Wellness program, we have constructed and repaired over 100 boreholes in the islands of Bussi, Zinga, Kituufu, and Kavanyanja—the four islands that make up Bussi Subcounty. These islands are home to hundreds of thousands of people, and the need to provide these vulnerable communities with clean water remains significant. We urge you to support this cause so we can continue increasing access to clean water for island communities.

Our Goals

Safe Water Systems

Install safe water systems in rural communities to ensure clean and reliable water access.

Community Education

Educate the community on water conservation and hygiene practices for sustainable living.

Reduce Diseases

Reduce water-borne diseases by ensuring access to clean and safe drinking water.

Empower Leaders

Empower local leaders to maintain and manage water resources effectively.
