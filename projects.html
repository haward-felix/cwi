Our Projects

At the Community Wellness Initiative (CWI), we believe in transformative community work through sustainable projects. Below are some of the key projects we're involved in.

Amazzi Wellness Project

Objective: Providing clean and accessible water to communities in need.

The Amazzi Wellness Project focuses on building wells, rainwater harvesting systems, and ensuring sustainable access to clean drinking water for rural communities.

Progress: Over 30 wells have been built, providing clean water to over 10,000 individuals.

Financial Freedom Project

Objective: Empowering communities through agricultural and entrepreneurial initiatives.

This project focuses on sustainable agriculture and small-scale farming, such as piggery, maize farming, and manure production. We provide training and financial support to help families become self-sufficient.

Progress: Over 50 families are participating in this initiative, with more joining each year.

Eat Green Walk Green

Objective: Promoting sustainable farming practices and eco-friendly lifestyles.

This project teaches communities how to grow sustainable crops, recycle waste, and live environmentally conscious lives. The goal is to improve food security while protecting the environment.

Progress: Community gardens have been established in 10 villages, benefiting hundreds of families.

Donate Now Volunteer

Lumbering & Eco-Tourism

Objective: Ensuring sustainable land use while promoting eco-tourism in the region.

This project focuses on balancing the need for natural resources with environmental conservation. By engaging in sustainable lumbering practices and promoting eco-tourism, we are able to create jobs while preserving the environment.

Progress: We've worked with local governments to protect over 1,000 hectares of forest land.
